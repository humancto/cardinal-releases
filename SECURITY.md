# Security & private disclosure

Cardinal is a free Mac app maintained by one person. If you find a security issue, an accessibility blocker tied to a specific kid's profile, or anything else you'd rather not say publicly — please use **GitHub Private Vulnerability Reporting**.

## How to report privately

1. Go to [**Open a private security advisory**](https://github.com/humancto/cardinal-releases/security/advisories/new).
2. GitHub account required (free). Sign in.
3. Fill in the form. The maintainer is the only person who sees it.
4. We respond within 3 business days. Acknowledgement first, then triage.

Despite the security-flavored name, GitHub Private Vulnerability Reporting works for any private-disclosure note:

- A specific accessibility blocker tied to your kid's profile
- A beta-program inquiry
- A consent-deletion request (per the [observation protocol](https://github.com/humancto/cardinal/blob/main/docs/observation-protocol.md))
- Anything you don't want public

## What's in scope

- The shipped Cardinal app and its DMG
- The Sparkle auto-update channel
- The landing page at humancto.github.io/cardinal-releases
- The observation protocol and beta-program privacy commitments

## What's out of scope

- Issues with GitHub itself (report to [GitHub Security](https://github.com/security))
- Third-party dependencies (we'll triage and refer upstream where appropriate)
- Bugs that aren't security/privacy sensitive — those go to public Issues with the bug-report template

## Privacy commitments

- No personal email exposed in the app binary or in PUBLIC source files.
- Private reports stay private. Aggregated themes may inform Cardinal's roadmap but no individual report is quoted, screenshotted, or shared without your written re-consent.
- The observation protocol commits Cardinal to no-audio/no-video/no-kid-identifiers data collection. Same standard applies to anything you send privately.

## For public bug reports + feature requests

Use the regular Issue templates: [https://github.com/humancto/cardinal-releases/issues/new/choose](https://github.com/humancto/cardinal-releases/issues/new/choose). Both templates have privacy-acknowledgement gates (no kid names, photos, voices in screenshots).
